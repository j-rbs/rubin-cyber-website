Foundations Implementation

Turning the roadmap into real changes: strong identity, protected devices, dependable backups and enough visibility to know when something’s wrong.

What we focus on

The goal of this stage is to give you a sensible baseline — not a perfect, over-engineered setup that nobody can maintain. We start from what you already have and improve configuration, coverage and day-to-day practices.

Typical implementation areas

  • MFA & SSO: rolling out strong authentication, with particular attention to admin and high-risk accounts.
  • Access hygiene: least privilege, joiner/mover/leaver flows, and dealing with shared accounts.
  • Endpoint protection: hardening and monitoring for laptops and desktops to a realistic baseline.
  • Backups: ensuring you actually have restorable backups for critical systems and data.
  • Logging & alerts: getting enough signal so incidents don’t quietly slip by.

Milestone based · typically 3–8 weeks depending on scope

How the work is structured

  • We break the roadmap into small, clear milestones with explicit owners.
  • We agree who does what: your team, me, and any vendors you already use.
  • We document simple runbooks and checks so changes are repeatable.
  • We keep leadership updated in plain language — progress, risks, trade-offs.

Tools and vendors

I’m vendor-neutral. Where possible, we make better use of what you already pay for. When new tools are needed, they’re chosen for fit and maintainability, not just features.

You stay in control — I’m there to guide, implement and de-risk.
